The Ultimate 3-Day Push/Pull/Legs Workout Routine

Published on June 4, 2026 | By Trainer Anupa Herath

If you're looking for a highly efficient, balanced, and time-tested workout structure, look no further than the **Push/Pull/Legs (PPL)** routine. By grouping muscles together based on their movement patterns, PPL allows you to train your entire body effectively while giving each muscle group plenty of time to rest and recover.

Here is our recommended 3-Day split for beginners and intermediates looking to build strength and lean muscle mass at FitZone Kurunegala.

The 3-Day PPL Workout Structure

Day 1: PUSH (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

Focuses on upper-body muscles that perform pushing movements to move weight away from your body.

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Time
Barbell Flat Bench Press 4 8 - 10 2 minutes
Dumbbell Overhead Shoulder Press 3 10 - 12 90 seconds
Incline Dumbbell Chest Flys 3 12 60 seconds
Tricep Rope Pushdowns 3 12 - 15 60 seconds

Day 2: PULL (Back, Biceps, Forearms)

Focuses on upper-body muscles that perform pulling movements to move weight towards your body.

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Time
Lat Pulldowns or Pull-ups 4 8 - 10 90 seconds
Seated Cable Rows 3 10 - 12 90 seconds
Dumbbell Bicep Hammer Curls 3 12 60 seconds
Barbell Bicep Curls 3 12 60 seconds

Day 3: LEGS (Quads, Hamstrings, Calves, Abs)

Focuses on the entire lower body. Don't skip leg day — lower body strength is critical for balance and core stability!

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Time
Barbell Back Squats 4 8 - 10 2 minutes
Lying Leg Curls 3 12 90 seconds
Leg Press 3 10 - 12 90 seconds
Standing Calf Raises 3 15 60 seconds
Hanging Leg Raises (Abs) 3 15 60 seconds
"Progressive overload is key. Write down the weights you use, and aim to add either 1 rep or a tiny bit of weight in your next session. Track your growth!"
